Swiss Design Lineage — History & Principles

Overview

The Swiss (International Typographic) style emerged in Switzerland in the 1940s–1960s. It emphasizes clarity, objectivity, and typographic precision. Key traits include asymmetric layouts, a strong grid, use of sans-serif type (e.g., Helvetica), clear hierarchy, and restrained color palettes.

Key Principles

  • Grid-driven layouts and modular structure
  • Sans-serif typography for neutrality and legibility
  • Asymmetry and intentional white space
  • Minimal color — limited palette with strong contrasts

Notable Figures & References

  1. Josef Müller‑Brockmann — grid and constructive graphic design
  2. Max Bill — typographic modernism and clarity
  3. Armin Hofmann — education and visual language
