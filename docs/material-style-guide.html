üéØ What Is Material Design?

Material Design is Google's design language that combines the rational grid systems of Swiss design with Bauhaus craft and physical materials. It treats interfaces as surfaces in 3D space using light, shadow, motion, and a robust token system (color, spacing, elevation, typography).

  • Digital surfaces behave like physical materials
  • Motion reveals relationships and hierarchy
  • Bold color creates visual delight
  • Grid-based layouts with intentional white space

Origins: Created by Google in 2014 under Matias Duarte as a response to flat UI trends; Material evolves through v1 (2014) ‚Üí v2 (2018) ‚Üí v3 (2021, "Material You").

Philosophy: "A material metaphor is the unifying theory of a rationalized space and a system of motion." ‚Äî Material Guidelines
